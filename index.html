Introduction

The mission and vision of the Center to Stream Healthcare In Place(C2SHIP) is to engage academic and industrial partners in joint efforts to promote in-home healthcare technologies that allow people to manage chronic diseases in their own homes. C2SHIP also seeks to accelerate innovation, multidisciplinary collaborations, and resource sharing, as well as prepare a workforce able to promote wellness through self-care technologies. C2SHIP involves strong partnerships with some of the leading academic institutions, including Caltech, University of Southern California, University of Arizona, Baylor School of Medicine, University of Missouri, and University of North-Dakota. Additionally, C2SHIP have 21 industrial partners, further enhancing the capacity to drive meaningful change in the field of in-home healthcare technologies. Together, we are all committed to making a significant and positive impact to revolutionaze the way chronic diseases are managed and treated.
For more information about C2SHIP please visit the website C2SHIP.org.

C2SHIP student and trainee organization (C2SHIP-STO) is dedicated to fostering connections and networking opportunities for undergraduate and graduate students, as well as postdoctoral researchers and research assistants. Our primary objective is to facilitate meaningful interactions between these aspiring professionals and esteemed figures in academia, industry, and federal agencies such as FDA, NIH, and NSF.
As part of the C2SHIP annual meeting, C2SHIP-STO is organizing a compelling poster session, providing a platform for students and trainees to showcase their research and project ideas to academic professors, industrial members, and representatives from federal agencies. This unique opportunity allows researchers to present their work in diverse fields like healthcare, engineering, cyber-security, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, all of which align with C2SHIP's mission.

Call for Abstracts

Topics

In this annual meeting, we invite the researchers to submit the abstract around the following topic areas:
  • Design wearable sensors/manufacturing
  • Machine learning\artificial intelligence
  • Data streaming and processing
  • Cyber Security/Feedback
  • Education and Societal Impact of in-place technologies
  • Mathematical modeling of medical data
  • Image processing and Computer Vision
  • Other
    • Note: If the research idea does not fall under any of the above topics but follows the principle of stream healthcare in place please submit them under “Other”.
